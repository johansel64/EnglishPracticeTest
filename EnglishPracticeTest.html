📚 Study Guide

Present Progressive (Presente Continuo)

📌 Structure:

Subject + am/is/are + verb-ing

📊 Conjugation:

Subject To Be Example
I am I am working
You are You are studying
He/She/It is She is playing
We are We are eating
They are They are running

✅ Affirmative:

I am playing / She is working / They are studying

❌ Negative:

Subject + am/is/are + NOT + verb-ing
I am not sleeping / He is not watching TV / We are not eating

❓ Question:

Am/Is/Are + subject + verb-ing?
Are you studying? / Is she working? / Are they playing?

📝 Use:

Actions happening RIGHT NOW or at this moment.

I am studying English right now.

Simple Present (Presente Simple)

📌 Structure:

Subject + verb (base form)
He/She/It + verb + S/ES

📊 Conjugation:

Subject Verb Example
I work I work every day
You work You work hard
He/She/It works She works at ICE
We work We work together
They work They work on Mondays

✅ Affirmative:

I play soccer / She plays piano / They eat vegetables

❌ Negative:

I/You/We/They + DO NOT (don't) + verb
He/She/It + DOES NOT (doesn't) + verb
I don't like coffee / He doesn't work on Sundays

❓ Question:

DO + I/you/we/they + verb?
DOES + he/she/it + verb?
Do you speak English? / Does she live here?

⚠️ Important:

Subject Auxiliary
I, You, We, They DO / DON'T
He, She, It DOES / DOESN'T

📝 Use:

Habits, routines, facts, and general truths.

I work at ICE / She studies English every day

Was / Were (Pasado del verbo To Be)

📌 Structure:

Subject + was/were

📊 Conjugation:

Subject Past Example
I was I was happy
You were You were there
He/She/It was She was tired
We were We were late
They were They were friends

⚠️ Key Rule:

WAS WERE
I, He, She, It You, We, They

✅ Affirmative:

I was at home / They were happy / It was cold

❌ Negative:

Subject + was/were + NOT
I wasn't ready / They weren't there

❓ Question:

Was/Were + subject?
Was she sick? / Were you at the party?

📝 Use:

Describe situations or states in the past.

The movie was interesting / We were at the beach yesterday

Will vs Be Going To (Futuro)

🔵 WILL

Subject + WILL + verb (base)

📝 Use WILL for:

1. Spontaneous decisions (decisiones espontáneas)

The phone is ringing. I will answer it.

2. Predictions without evidence (predicciones sin evidencia)

I think it will rain tomorrow.

3. Promises (promesas)

I will help you with your homework.

🟢 BE GOING TO

Subject + am/is/are + GOING TO + verb

📝 Use BE GOING TO for:

1. Plans already decided (planes ya decididos)

We bought tickets. We are going to travel next week.

2. Predictions with evidence (predicciones con evidencia)

Look at those clouds! It is going to rain.

⚖️ Comparison:

Situation Use
Plan ya decidido BE GOING TO
Decisión espontánea WILL
Predicción con evidencia BE GOING TO
Predicción sin evidencia WILL
Promesa WILL

✅ Examples:

I am going to visit my grandmother. (plan)
I will call you later. (promesa)

Countable / Uncountable Nouns

✅ COUNTABLE (Contables)

Se pueden contar: 1 apple, 2 apples, 3 apples

Examples:

apple, book, car, chair, computer, pen, phone, table

Use with:

Word Example
a / an a book, an apple
many many books
few / a few a few apples
How many? How many books?

❌ UNCOUNTABLE (Incontables)

NO se pueden contar: water (no "1 water, 2 waters")

Examples:

water, rice, sugar, milk, money, coffee, bread, information

Use with:

Word Example
some some water
much much money
little / a little a little sugar
How much? How much water?

⚠️ Common Mistakes:

❌ I need a water → ✅ I need some water
❌ How many money? → ✅ How much money?
